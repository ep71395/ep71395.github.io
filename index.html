United Football Academy and My Journey

A Summary of my life with Soccer

My parents put me in a soccer club when I was around four or five years old. The club was called NSA and the name changed when I was in middle school. It's now called UFA. I was never really good at soccer when I was younger, but my parents thought of it as a good way to exercise. As I got older starting in middle school, my skills were improving significantly. I was always on the second team growing up, called Red team and in my eighth grade year I made it to the top team, called Premier . This was very exciting for me because I was on the best team in the academy of my age group. I remained on this team until I left the academy during my high school years.

As I stated already, I started out with the club when I was only four or five years old. The club was called Norcross Soccer Academy back then. The academy joined with a bigger club which resulted in the name change of UFA. You'll find more details about the differences between NSA and UFA here.

More on how the academy works

There are about five teams in each age group. The teams are divided by sex, therefore boys and girls seperately. There are team levels in each age group:

  1. Red
  2. Gold
  3. Black
  4. White
  5. Blue
